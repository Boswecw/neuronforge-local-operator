Rawn stood beneath the broken arch and listened to the wind moving through the stones. The sound was thin and strange, like something trying to speak through a cracked tooth. He didn't like it, but he said nothing because Amicae was already ahead of him and Rowetha was watching everything with those sharp, unblinking eyes of hers.

There were too many tracks in the mud, and not enough of them were fresh. Some had been left by boots, some by hooves, and one set looked almost human except for the way the heel dragged at the end of every step. Rawn crouched and studied them for a moment, then rose with a frown that he did not bother hiding.

"We're late," he said.

Amicae glanced back over her shoulder. "Late for what?"

He looked toward the ruin beyond the trees. "For whatever happened here."
