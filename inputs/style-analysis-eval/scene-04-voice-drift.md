Mira pressed her back against the cold stone and listened. The guards' footsteps had passed twice now, regular as clockwork, and she'd clocked the gap at roughly ninety seconds. Enough time, maybe. She was good at math when it counted.

She inched forward and peered around the corner. The corridor stretched ahead, empty. Her heart thudded against her ribs.

I have always been a careful person. From the time I was a child, I observed the world with a kind of deliberate attention that others found strange, as though the act of watching itself were a form of power. It was this quality that had led me here, to this cold stone passage, this moment of held breath and suspended action.

Mira moved fast when she moved, low and quiet, her soft-soled boots making almost no sound on the flagstones. She reached the door in under a minute. She tested the handle. Locked, obviously, because nothing was ever easy.

The young woman had not expected it to be easy. She had prepared for this. She had trained. One doesn't simply walk into the archive without preparation — she had spent weeks mapping the schedule, the patrol rotations, the window of exposure. Every variable had been accounted for.

She pulled the pick from her sleeve and went to work. Her fingers remembered the motion before her mind did. The tumblers shifted. She felt the catch give.

Mira pushed through the door and slipped into the dark.
