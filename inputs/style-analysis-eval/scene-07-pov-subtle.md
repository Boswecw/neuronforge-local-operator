Lena pressed the elevator button a second time, then a third, aware that this changed nothing. The lobby behind her was empty. The concierge desk stood unattended. She had twelve minutes before the auction closed, and the building's ancient elevator had apparently decided this was the moment to prove a point.

She pried open the stairwell door and started up. Sixth floor. She'd done worse. The stairwell smelled of cigarette smoke and old paint, the fluorescent light on the third-floor landing buzzing at a frequency that burrowed into the teeth. She kept moving.

Across town, Marcus had already made his decision, though neither of them knew it yet. He had withdrawn his bid and left the auction house without explanation, the lot going to whatever floor price the auctioneer could salvage.

Lena hit the sixth floor at a run and pushed through into the corridor. The auction room was at the end of the hall, its double doors still open. She could hear the auctioneer's voice, that practiced cadence of urgency and control. She wasn't too late. She smoothed her jacket and walked the last thirty feet at a pace that suggested she had never been in any particular hurry.
