"You shouldn't have come here," Adric said.

"You knew I would," Fennick replied.

"I hoped you wouldn't."

"Hope doesn't change much."

Adric turned away. Fennick waited.

"What do you want from me?"

"You know what I want."

"Say it anyway."

Fennick crossed his arms. "The ledger. The one from the Delacourt transfer."

"I burned it."

"You didn't."

"How would you know?"

"Because you don't burn things that might still be useful."

Adric was quiet for a moment. "You've been watching me."

"I've been careful."

"That's not the same thing."

"No," Fennick said, "but it gets the same result."

The room held the silence between them. Adric moved to the window.

"Even if I had it," Adric said, "what makes you think I'd give it to you?"

"I'm not asking you to give it to me."

"Then what are you asking?"

"I'm asking you to stop pretending you don't have it. We can negotiate from there."

Adric considered this. He looked out at the street below, the carts moving through the afternoon crowd, the ordinary motion of an ordinary day that had nothing to do with him or Fennick or the ledger that was absolutely not sitting in the locked drawer of his desk three feet away.

"Give me a day," he said finally.

"You have until morning," Fennick said, and left.
