Tomas walked into the room. The room was small. There was a table in the room. There were two chairs at the table. The chairs were wooden. Tomas sat in one of the chairs.

He looked at the table. There was a cup on the table. The cup had tea in it. The tea was cold. He did not drink the tea.

The door opened. A man came in. The man was tall. He sat in the other chair. He looked at Tomas.

"You came," the man said.

"I came," Tomas said.

The man picked up the cup. He looked at the tea. He put the cup down. He looked at Tomas again.

"We need to talk," the man said.

Tomas nodded. He waited. The man was quiet for a moment. The room was quiet. The light came through the window. It was afternoon light. It was pale.

"It's about the contract," the man said.

Tomas knew it would be. He had known it would be about the contract. He had thought about the contract all morning. He had thought about what he would say. He had thought about what the man would say. Now the man was here. Now they were going to talk about it.

"Tell me," Tomas said.
