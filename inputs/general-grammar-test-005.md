Rawn had intended to answer immediately, but the words did not come in any useful order. He understood what Amicae was asking, and that was part of the trouble. It was an easy thing to promise when standing in the dark with danger still far enough away to be discussed. It became a different thing entirely once the cost had a face.

"I am not saying no," he said at last.

Amicae's expression did not change. "That is often how people begin before they do."

He let out a breath that might have been a laugh if there had been any humor in him. "Then I will try again. I am saying I know what this would require, and I do not trust myself to pretend otherwise."

For a moment nobody spoke. Then Rowetha, quiet as a cat and twice as difficult to ignore, said, "That may be the first useful thing anyone has said tonight."
