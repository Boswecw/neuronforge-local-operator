# Lore Safe Test 002

Rawn Mortisimus crossed the lower court before dawn and paused beside the cracked basin where the acolytes once washed their hands. The stone was cold, the water black, and the old mark of Ith had not fully faded from the rim.

He did not like the stillness of the place. It sat badly with him, as if the courtyard were listening for a name no one should speak aloud. A lesser man might of turned back at the gate, but Rawn had seen too much of Eryndor's ruin to fear silence.

Near the archway, Rowetha knelt with one hand against the flagstones. "The Essence is wrong here," she said, though her voice were steady. "Not dead. Bound."

Rawn looked toward the sealed door below the chapel and rested his hand on the pommel of his sword. "Then we open it careful," he said. "And we do not give the Ithek what it wants."
