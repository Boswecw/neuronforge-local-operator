# Lore Safe Test 004

The hall beyond the shrine was not large, though it felt larger in the way ruined places sometimes do. Sound went strange in it. A whisper carried too far, while a bootstep seemed to stop just short of the ear.

Rowetha turned once in the doorway and frowned toward the far wall. "Did you hear that?"

Rawn had heard something, or thought he had, but the place had a manner of making certainty look foolish. "Maybe," he said.

That answer annoyed Amicae more than fear would of. She drew a slow breath and looked again at the cracked reliefs cut into the stone. The nearest figure had no face left, only a smooth place where one had been, and yet the posture was somehow accusing.

"We are being watched," she said.

"Perhaps," Rawn replied, though whether he meant the dead, the living, or merely memory was not clear even to him.
