# Lore Safe Test 006

The crypt beneath Saint Vey's had been sealed for one hundred years, which was reason enough to leave it shut. But reason had long since lost its place in Eryndor, and old doors were forever being opened by the desperate, the curious, and the damned.

The smell met them first. Not rot exactly, nor damp, but something older and thinner, like air that had forgotten how to belong among the living. Rowetha flinched as the last stone gave way. Amicae did not. Rawn could not tell whether that was courage or exhaustion.

Inside, the chamber was lined with narrow alcoves. Most were empty. A few still held shapes under cloth so decayed they looked more like settled shadow than burial shrouds. On the far wall, someone had written a prayer in ash, though prayer was perhaps too kind a word for it.

"It was made by a faithful hand," Rowetha said quietly.

"No," said Amicae. "By a frightened one."

Rawn read the final line and wished he had not. The words were plain enough, but plain words are often the ones that cut deepest when the dark has had time to wear them thin.
