The letter was short and polite enough on first reading, but the more Rawn looked at it the less he liked the shape of it. Nothing in the wording was openly threatening, and that somehow made it worse. A threat could be met. Courtesy like this had to be interpreted, and interpretation always left room for mistakes.

Amicae read it over his shoulder and gave it back without hurry. "He wants you uncertain," she said.

"He wants me obedient."

"No," she said. "If he wanted that, he would have sent soldiers. This is for making you choose the wrong thing by yourself."

Rowetha, who had been quiet longer than either of them liked, said, "Then maybe we should stop pretending the choice is between good and bad. It might just be between bad and worse."
