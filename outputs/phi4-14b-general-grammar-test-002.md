The inn was warmer than Rawn expected, but not by much. Smoke clung to the rafters, and the floor sloped just enough that every table appeared a little crooked if you stared at it too long. A woman with a scar across her chin brought stew without asking who had ordered it, and Rowetha eyed the bowl as though it might bite her first.

Amicae sat with her back to the wall, one hand wrapped around the spoon, even though she hadn't taken a bite yet. "Either these people are generous," she said quietly, "or they already know why we're here."

Rawn glanced around the room. No one seemed interested in them, which only made him trust the place less. "Eat first," he muttered. "Then worry."
