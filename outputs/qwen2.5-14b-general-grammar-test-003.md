By morning, the rain had stopped, but the camp still felt wet—dampening clothes, leather, and even tempers. Rawn had slept little and poorly. Every time he closed his eyes, he thought he heard footsteps circling just beyond the firelight. But when he rose to listen, there was only the sound of dripping branches and the soft shifting of the horses.

Rowetha was already awake, crouched near the ashes with her blanket hanging loosely around her shoulders. "Something came close in the night," she said.

Amicae looked up from where she was tightening the strap on her bracer. "Close enough to see?"

Rowetha nodded once. "Close enough to decide against it."
