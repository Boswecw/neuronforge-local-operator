By morning, the rain had stopped, but the camp still felt wet in a way that permeated clothes, leather, and temper alike. Rawn had slept little and badly; every time he shut his eyes, he thought he heard footsteps circling just beyond the firelight. When he rose to listen, there was nothing except dripping branches and the soft complaining shift of the horses.

Rowetha was already awake, crouched near the ashes with her blanket hanging loosely around her shoulders. "Something came close in the night," she said.

Amicae looked up from where she was tightening the strap on her bracer. "Close enough to see?"

Rowetha nodded once. "Close enough to decide against it."
