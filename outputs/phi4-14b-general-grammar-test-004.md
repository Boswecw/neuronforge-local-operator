The letter was short and polite enough at first glance, but the more Rawn examined it, the less he liked its implications. There was nothing openly threatening in the wording, which made it all the more unsettling. A threat could be confronted directly; however, this courteous tone had to be interpreted, leaving room for errors.

Amicae glanced over his shoulder and returned the letter without hurry. "He wants you uncertain," she said.

"He wants me obedient."

"No," she replied. "If he wanted that, he would have sent soldiers. This is meant to lead you to choose incorrectly on your own."

Rowetha, who had been quiet longer than either of them preferred, finally spoke up. "Then maybe we should stop pretending the choice is between good and bad. It might just be a matter of choosing the lesser evil."
