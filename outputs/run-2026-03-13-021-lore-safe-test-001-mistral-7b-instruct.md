 Rawn Mortisimus stood beneath the blackened rise and listened to the wind worry the old boards of the mill. The path behind him was half mud and half memory, and each step he took felt like a trespass he had already committed.

Aeloria had warned him that the land around Eryndor was not as dead as it appeared, but the quiet here did not feel alive either. It hung in the air like a held breath, thin and old and full of something watchful.

He thought of Amicae, and of the anger she carried as if it were a second blade. Rowetha had said little on the road, yet her silence spoke volumes. The child saw more than any child should have seen, and the thought of it weighed heavily on him.

There was Essence in this place still, though not in any healthy form. Something in it had gone wrong long before they arrived, and now the corrupted Essence moved through the grass and stone with a patience that made his skin crawl.
