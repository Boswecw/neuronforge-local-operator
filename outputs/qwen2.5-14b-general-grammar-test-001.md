Rawn stood beneath the broken arch and listened to the wind moving through the stones. The sound was thin and strange, like something trying to speak through a cracked tooth. Though he didn't like it, he said nothing because Amicae was already ahead of him, and Rowetha was watching everything with her sharp, unblinking eyes.

There were too many tracks in the mud, none of them particularly fresh. Some had been left by boots, some by hooves, and one set appeared almost human except for the way the heel dragged at the end of every step. Rawn crouched to study them briefly before rising with a frown he didn't bother hiding.

"We're late," he said.

Amicae glanced back over her shoulder. "Late for what?"

He looked toward the ruin beyond the trees. "For whatever happened here."
