# Lore Safe Test 005

By the time they reached the burned watchtower, the snow had thinned to a gray crust over the stones. What remained of the upper chamber was open to the sky, and the iron stair along the inner wall shook whenever the wind touched it.

Amicae went first, light on her feet despite the cold. Rawn followed with less grace but more caution, one hand near the rail and the other free in case the old structure decided to give way beneath them.

At the top, Rowetha was already kneeling beside a scatter of soot-black bones. She did not touch them. "These were not left here," she said. "They were arranged."

Rawn looked down at the pattern and felt a slow unease settle into him. The circles were too deliberate, the gaps too measured. Whoever had done this knew exactly what they were asking the dark to hear.

"Then we do not answer," he said.
