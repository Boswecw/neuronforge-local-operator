Rawn Mortisimus stood beneath the blackened rise and listened to the wind worry the old boards of the mill. The path behind him was half mud and half memory, and each step had felt like a trespass he had already made before.

Aeloria had warned him that the land around Eryndor was not as dead as it looked, but the quiet here did not feel living either. It hung in the air like a held breath, thin and old and full of something watchful.

He thought of Amicae, and of the anger she carried as if it was a second blade. Rowetha had said little on the road, yet her silence spoke enough. The child had seen more than any child should have seen, and the thought of it sat badly in him.

There was Essence in this place still, though not in any wholesome form. Something in it had gone wrong long before they arrived, and now the corrupted Essence moved through the grass and stone with a patience that made his skin crawl.
